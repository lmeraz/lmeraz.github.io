## The Problem
Let's face it. No one has a perfect childhood. We are a product of our elements. We each a beautiful art piece put together with all means available to us at the time. It's great to reflect on what the art of us has to say. It took an open mind to be curious about my past and see it as face value. Though it was not perfect, I made the most of it. Now, older in age, I see myself performing acts of kindness to myself and giving back to as past me that sure as hell will be proud to know what I'm up to now. There's some advice I take to heart for myself and some advice I offer to parents, so their children are ready to face our world today.

## Manage your own baggage
It's important to know what you are dealing with yourself. Understand that it's your job to have a positive relationship with your own self. That's frankly the only level we have available to us. Changing our perspective on our circumstances and taking responsibility for what's ahead. It's unfair for a child fresh to this world to be exposed to your own bag. They're tabula rasa, with so much potential ahead. Why should they carry your bag of shit?

## Don't confuse chronic unpredictable toxic stress with childhood challenges that foster resilience
Simply put, you are not making children resilient to stress by being unpredictable. Children need a safe environment to explore their surroundings and take risks. When you don't make any fucking sense, children suffer. They don't know how to react, and often, they'll learn to reach in an unhealthy or unproductive way to cope with your bullshit.

## Instill the 4 S's in your children
The 4 S's are Safe, Security, Seen, and Soothe. These concepts are initially conceived to help parents create loving bonds with their children. Being safe requires physical and emotional safety. Use soft tones of voice, and I statement to bring up difficult topics. When we don't feel safe, we'll fight, flee, or withdraw. We can also increase the feeling of safety by being vulnerable, hey who knew, parents can also be vulnerable with their kids? Security is a sense of safety combined with stability. THought there may be ebbs and flows in the relationship, we know that there's a consistency and soon things will be back to normal. If parents act on extremes, it leaves very little security for the child. Arguments can b3 resolved calmly. You and your child will both be thankful. Children need to feel seen by their parents. It helps with building the child's sense of self. Remember, they just started life, you may be able to give them insights to feelings and emotions they've yet to identify or know the name of for that matter. SUrprise, what you're doing is empathizing with your child. If you're having a hard time, merely mirroring back or paraphrasing what the child is saying to you will be significantly appreciated by your kid. In soothing, it's essential to make positive physical contact every day to soothe each other. Not much changes from us as babies, crying and waiting for an adult to soother away our discomfort. 


## Look into your Child's Eye
We communicate so many non-verbals through the eyes. At a young age, I personally looked away from anyone's eyes. I was always afraid people cast judgment on me only with their eyes. It was later in life I learned this was self prophesizing, and I was often making assumptions that were simply not true. As I gained confidence in looking at eyes, they cast so much emotion, it should never be left to guesswork. Reveal what your eyes have to say.

## If you lose it, apologize right away
Sure there will be times when mindfulness goes out the window. It's important to own up to our errors. Take responsibility for your mistakes. Fucking apologize. Be an adult, you know better.

## Validate and normalize all of your child's emotions
All emotions are valid emotions if expressed by a human being. You cannot deprive someone of how they feel. The buddha says everyone suffers, and it's true. Sure there are varying degrees of suffering. You may disagree with ones' perspective on what pain entails, but you should not heed judgment.


## Amplify the Good Feelings
We often dwell on the negative emotions and feelings. How often, however, have you celebrater the happy and pleasant moments? We focus on the bad as a reminder on how to avoid it in the future - thanks to evolution. It's now in our evolutionary nature to success to focus on the good and then reach out for those types of moments again.


## Stop, look, go
When an emotion arises, stop what you are doing. Look at what led to that emotion. Then act. You'll come to recognize your time to buffer a reactionary nature and bring you closer to the clarity of mind.


## Give a name to difficult emotions
Here's where I struggled most. What title to give that feeling that I didn't understand? I don't know. I didn't know for a long time. It took a trusted confidant to share with them how I felt. Together we arrived at the answer. Sometimes it was anguish, other times, anger, embarrassment. Giving the unknown a known name eased confusion and settled many different emotions. FOr a while, all I was able to label emotions are was 'uncomfortable,' and now I have a full dictionary to identify and explore the feelings I feel.

## The incredible power of the twenty-second hug
We are social creatures. All of us, to some degree, express ourselves with touch. One of the most effective tools in sharing our empathy and nurturing and protecting someone is by offering a hug. Twenty seconds seem too long on the giving end? The receiving end will much appreciate it and take comfort in your sheltering arms.

## make "what's happening" a safe and open conversation
You need to have conversations with no judgement attached. If there is any judgement, you can guarantee the truth won't come out. It's not safe to do so. You provided a hostile environment. You have to be prepared with an open mind. Do not come into the conversation with your own preconceptions, assumptions, or expectations. A chat free of judgment leads to a more fruitful one.

## A child needs a reliable adult  or mentor
Children look to their elders to borrow and gain wisdom. They can experiment and possibly veer of a pah. Or you can help shape that path, saving them from committing your own mistakes. ANd leading them to the correct answers. Don't know the answer? Be honest and share, at the least, provide guidance on where the answer may exist.

## Bring mindfulness to schools
Mindfulness doesn't need to stay at home. FOster it in school and other activities. It's an everyday commitment to a healthy habit.
